SAP C4C — Lead to Opportunity to Quote
Business Process Documentation

Company: Trelleborg Sealing Solutions  |  Department: Sales EMEA  |  Process Owner: Head of Inside Sales  |  Version: 2.4  |  Last Updated: March 2026

1. Process Overview

This document describes the end-to-end sales process from initial lead capture through to a submitted customer quote, as executed by the EMEA sales team using SAP Cloud for Customer (C4C). The process involves three core phases — Lead Management, Opportunity Development, and Quote Generation — and spans multiple roles, systems, and approval levels.

Average cycle time from lead creation to submitted quote: 12–18 business days
Average number of manual handoffs per cycle: 9
Percentage of quotes requiring rework: 34%

4. Phase 1 — Lead Management

Step 1.1 — Lead Creation

Marketing Marketo → SAP C4C

Leads are created in SAP C4C through three channels:

  1. Marketo sync — inbound web form completions, whitepaper downloads, event registrations push to C4C automatically. Fields mapped: First name, Last name, Email, Company, Country, Campaign source. Phone, job title, and budget fields are NOT mapped and must be added manually.
  2. Manual entry — trade show business cards, referrals, and cold outreach are entered manually by Inside Sales into C4C Lead screen. Average time: 8–12 minutes per lead.
  3. Import from Excel — batch imports from external list providers. ISR downloads CSV, maps fields manually, imports via C4C import tool. Error rate: ~18% requiring post-import correction.
Marketo to C4C sync is one-way and only fires on form submission. If a contact updates their details in Marketo, C4C is not updated automatically. Sales reps frequently work with stale data.
No automatic duplicate detection on import. ISRs manually search for existing accounts before creating leads, taking 3–5 minutes per lead. Duplicate leads are discovered during quarterly data clean-up.
Step 1.2 — Lead Scoring & Prioritisation

Inside Sales Rep SAP C4C, Excel

C4C has a built-in lead score field (1–100) but it is NOT automatically populated. The ISR manually reviews each new lead and assigns a score based on:

  • Company size (revenue/employees — looked up manually on LinkedIn or company website)
  • Industry fit (manufacturing, aerospace, automotive = high priority)
  • Engagement signals (email opens, page visits — checked in Marketo, not visible in C4C)
  • Geography (EMEA vs. other regions)
  • Budget indication (if mentioned in form or email)
Lead scoring is entirely manual and subjective. There is no scoring model or algorithm. Two ISRs may score the same lead differently by 30–40 points. High-value leads are sometimes deprioritised due to workload, not fit.
Engagement data (email opens, website visits) lives in Marketo and is not visible in C4C. ISRs must switch between two systems to get a full picture of a lead's intent.
Estimated time: 15–25 minutes per lead for research and scoring
Step 1.3 — Initial Outreach & Qualification

Inside Sales Rep Outlook, SAP C4C

ISR contacts the lead via email or phone within the target SLA of 24 hours (actual average: 38 hours). Qualification follows the BANT framework:

  • Budget — Does the prospect have an approved budget for this category?
  • Authority — Is this person the decision-maker, or do we need to reach the economic buyer?
  • Need — Is there a confirmed technical or business requirement?
  • Timeline — What is the expected purchase timeline?

ISR records the qualification outcome in C4C under the Lead Activity log. Email correspondence is manually copied into C4C notes (Outlook plugin captures only ~60% of emails due to sync issues).

The Outlook-C4C plugin frequently fails to log emails, requiring manual copy-paste of email content into C4C notes. ISRs spend an estimated 45 minutes per day on email logging.
There are no qualification call scripts or guided prompts inside C4C. New ISRs rely on printed checklists. Qualification quality varies significantly by rep.
If BANT is met (≥3 of 4 criteria): proceed to Step 1.4 (convert to Opportunity). If not met: return to nurture sequence in Marketo and mark lead as "Nurture" in C4C.
Step 1.4 — Lead Qualification Review & Conversion

Inside Sales Rep Sales Manager SAP C4C

Qualified leads are reviewed in the weekly Monday pipeline call (60–90 minutes). Sales Manager approves conversion. ISR converts lead to opportunity in C4C using the "Convert Lead" action, which:

  • Creates a new Opportunity record linked to the Account and Contact
  • Copies lead source, industry, and ISR notes to the opportunity
  • Does NOT copy custom fields (product interest, technical requirements) — these must be re-entered manually
Custom field data entered during lead qualification is lost on conversion and must be re-entered on the opportunity. ISRs frequently forget to do this, leading to incomplete opportunity records.
Lead to conversion cycle: average 5 business days after first contact

5. Phase 2 — Opportunity Management

Step 2.1 — Opportunity Creation & Assignment

Inside Sales Rep Field Sales Rep SAP C4C

New opportunity is populated with:

  • Opportunity name (manual — convention: "[Company] – [Product Category] – [Year]")
  • Expected revenue (estimated by ISR — often a rough guess at this stage)
  • Close date (defaulted to 90 days — frequently not updated)
  • Sales stage (set to "1 – Identified")
  • Product line (selected from drop-down — product-level detail added later)
  • Assigned FSR (based on geography — ISR checks territory map in SharePoint)

FSR is notified by email. No automated alert exists in C4C for opportunity assignment.

Territory assignment is done by consulting a SharePoint spreadsheet, not automated logic in C4C. When sales territories change (quarterly), the SharePoint file is updated manually and is often out of date for 2–3 weeks.
Step 2.2 — Customer Discovery & Requirements Gathering

Field Sales Rep Outlook, Teams/Zoom, SharePoint, SAP C4C

FSR schedules a discovery call or site visit. Outcomes captured:

  • Technical specifications (application, media, pressure, temperature, dimensions)
  • Volume requirements (annual units or spend)
  • Competitive landscape (incumbent supplier, price reference)
  • Decision timeline and stakeholders
  • Relevant industry standards or certifications required

FSR takes notes in Outlook, Word, or sometimes handwritten, then manually transcribes into C4C activity log and uploads any documents to SharePoint. SharePoint link is manually pasted into C4C notes field.

Meeting notes are captured in multiple places (Outlook notes, Word doc, Teams chat transcript, handwritten). Consolidation into C4C is manual and inconsistent. Key technical requirements are frequently missing from the CRM record.
No structured requirements template exists in C4C. Each FSR captures different levels of detail, making pipeline reviews inconsistent.
Discovery phase: 2–5 business days including scheduling, meeting, and documentation
Step 2.3 — Opportunity Stages & Milestone Tracking

Field Sales Rep SAP C4C

The EMEA sales team uses a 5-stage opportunity pipeline in C4C:

StageDescriptionTarget Win ProbabilityExit Criteria
1 – IdentifiedLead converted, initial contact made10%Customer confirms interest, discovery call scheduled
2 – QualifiedBANT confirmed, FSR assigned25%Technical requirements documented
3 – Solution DesignProduct/solution identified, pricing requested50%Pricing team engaged, draft solution ready
4 – ProposalQuote submitted to customer75%Customer acknowledged receipt, verbal feedback received
5 – NegotiationCommercial terms under discussion90%PO received or opportunity closed lost
Stage progression is manually updated by the FSR. There are no automated stage-change triggers or mandatory field requirements at each stage gate. FSRs often skip stages or back-date entries ahead of pipeline reviews. Win probability is not statistically calibrated — it is set manually by the rep.
Step 2.4 — Internal Technical Review

Field Sales Rep Technical Sales Engineer Outlook, SharePoint

For non-standard products or complex applications, FSR emails the Technical Sales Engineering (TSE) team with customer requirements. TSE reviews and provides:

  • Product recommendation (SAP material number + description)
  • Technical feasibility confirmation
  • Lead time estimate (cross-checked with SAP ERP manually)
  • Any required testing or certifications

TSE response is provided via email. FSR copies relevant content into C4C opportunity notes.

The entire TSE review happens outside C4C via email. Response time is 3–7 business days with no SLA tracking. TSE workload is not visible; requests queue in a shared mailbox with no priority system.
Product recommendations from TSE often reference SAP ERP material numbers, which FSRs must then search for manually in C4C's product catalogue (since ERP-to-C4C sync is nightly and incomplete).
TSE review: average 4 business days
Step 2.5 — Opportunity Approval for High-Value Deals

Field Sales Rep Sales Manager SAP C4C, Email

Opportunities with estimated value >€50,000 require Sales Manager approval before a quote can be issued. FSR submits an internal approval request by:

  1. Updating the opportunity to stage "3 – Solution Design"
  2. Sending a manual email to the Sales Manager with a summary (company, product, volume, estimated revenue, competitive situation, recommended approach)
  3. Sales Manager reviews, may request additional information, and replies by email with approval or instructions
  4. FSR notes approval in C4C activity log
There is no formal approval workflow in C4C. Approval is tracked via email thread, not in the CRM. Auditing approval history requires searching Outlook, not C4C. If the Sales Manager is on holiday, requests wait with no escalation path.

6. Phase 3 — Quote Generation

Step 3.1 — Pricing Request to Pricing Team

Field Sales Rep Pricing Team Email, Excel, SAP ERP

FSR sends a pricing request to the regional Pricing Team via email, attaching:

  • Customer name and country
  • Product material numbers (from TSE recommendation)
  • Annual volume estimate (units or EUR)
  • Target price or competitive reference (if known)
  • Required delivery terms (Incoterms)
  • Customer-specific certifications or packaging requirements

Pricing Team accesses SAP ERP SD module to retrieve base pricing conditions, applies customer-specific discount scales, calculates margins, and returns a price recommendation via email with an Excel attachment.

Pricing calculations are done entirely in a standalone Excel workbook maintained by the Pricing Team. There are 6 different versions of the pricing model across 3 EMEA regions with inconsistent formulas. Prices returned to FSRs sometimes include errors due to manual formula overrides.
No pricing history per customer is visible in C4C. FSRs cannot see what prices were offered in previous quotes without searching old emails or SAP ERP (to which most FSRs do not have access).
Pricing turnaround: 2–4 business days (standard); 5–8 days for custom/non-catalogue products
Step 3.2 — Quote Creation in SAP C4C

Field Sales Rep SAP C4C

FSR creates a quote in SAP C4C linked to the opportunity:

  1. Open opportunity → "Create Quote" action
  2. Add line items: select products from C4C catalogue (note: catalogue may be up to 24 hours out of date vs. SAP ERP)
  3. Manually enter prices received from Pricing Team (no direct feed from ERP pricing to C4C quote)
  4. Enter quantities, delivery dates, Incoterms
  5. Add validity period (default 30 days — sometimes extended manually without re-approving pricing)
  6. Add customer-specific T&Cs reference (copied from a standard Word template, pasted into notes)
  7. Set payment terms (checked against customer master in SAP ERP — separate login required)
Prices must be manually typed from the Pricing Team's Excel into C4C — no integration between ERP pricing conditions and C4C quote lines. Transcription errors are common. Quote rework due to pricing errors accounts for 28% of quote cycle time.
Product descriptions in C4C are often truncated or in German (the ERP default language). FSRs manually edit descriptions for English-language quotes, introducing inconsistency.
Step 3.3 — Discount Approval

Field Sales Rep Sales Manager Pricing Team Email, SAP C4C

If the proposed price is below the standard list price by more than the FSR's discount authority:

Discount LevelApproverProcess
0–5%FSR (self-approved)No approval required
5–15%Sales ManagerEmail request with justification
15–25%Sales DirectorEmail + Excel P&L sheet attached
>25%VP Sales + CFO sign-offFormal memo + finance review (5–10 days)
Discount approval is email-based with no workflow, no SLA, and no audit trail in C4C. Approvals are frequently delayed when approvers are travelling. No escalation mechanism exists. Some FSRs bypass the process by splitting quote lines to keep each line below the threshold.
Step 3.4 — Quote Document Generation

Field Sales Rep SAP C4C, Word, Outlook

C4C generates a quote PDF using a standard template. However, the standard C4C quote template is not used in most cases because:

  • The C4C template does not support multi-currency display
  • The template lacks company logo customisation by country
  • T&Cs are too generic and require legal customisation for large accounts

In practice, ~70% of quotes are produced by copying C4C quote data into a Word template maintained on SharePoint. FSR fills in all fields manually, generates PDF, and attaches to the C4C quote record.

The Word template approach creates a parallel document that is not connected to C4C data. When pricing changes after customer feedback, the FSR must update both the C4C quote record AND the Word document. Version control is managed by renaming files (e.g., "QuoteAcme_v3_FINAL_approved.docx").
Step 3.5 — Legal T&C Review (Large Accounts)

Legal / Contracts Field Sales Rep SharePoint, Outlook, Word

For accounts with annual value >€150k, the Legal team reviews T&Cs before submission. FSR emails Legal with:

  • Draft quote document
  • Customer's own T&C document (if customer has provided one)
  • Any special commercial conditions requested by the customer
Legal review takes 5–10 business days. There is no template for standard commercial exceptions, so similar situations are reviewed from scratch each time. Legal frequently requests information already in C4C, as they do not have C4C access.
Step 3.6 — Quote Submission to Customer

Field Sales Rep Outlook, SAP C4C

FSR emails the quote PDF to the customer contact. After sending:

  1. C4C quote status is manually updated to "Sent"
  2. A follow-up activity is manually created in C4C (reminder call in 5 business days)
  3. FSR notes the customer contact who received the quote in the activity log

Customer opens and reads the email outside of any system — there is no read-receipt or portal tracking.

There is no way to know if the customer has opened or read the quote. FSRs call customers to confirm receipt, often wasting time when the email went to spam or the customer is travelling.
Follow-up scheduling is manual. If the FSR is ill or leaves the company, pending follow-ups are not visible to the replacement FSR without checking individual Outlook calendars.
Step 3.7 — Customer Feedback & Quote Revision

Field Sales Rep Pricing Team Outlook, SAP C4C, Excel

Customer provides feedback via email or phone. Common revision scenarios:

  • Price negotiation — customer requests lower price → discount approval loop (Step 3.3)
  • Product change — customer specifies different variant → TSE re-engagement (Step 2.4) + re-pricing (Step 3.1)
  • Volume change — customer revises volume up or down → re-pricing
  • Scope addition — customer adds product lines → full cycle for new lines

Each revision creates a new version of the quote in C4C. Previous versions are kept but there is no automated change log. FSR must manually note what changed between versions in the activity log.

34% of quotes require at least one revision. Average revision cycle: 3–5 business days. Total elapsed time from first quote submission to customer acceptance averages 22 business days for complex deals.

8. Known Issues & Ongoing Problems

  • The C4C-to-ERP sync runs nightly. Real-time stock and pricing information is not available in C4C, causing quotes to be issued for products that are out of stock or at incorrect prices.
  • There is no AI-assisted forecasting. Sales Managers build quarterly forecasts by manually reviewing all open opportunities and applying their own judgement to win probabilities.
  • Customer communication history is split between Outlook (emails), Teams (calls/chats), and C4C (manually logged activities). There is no unified view of all customer interactions.
  • New FSRs take an average of 3 months to reach productivity due to the complexity of the manual process and multiple systems. There is no guided onboarding within C4C.
  • There is no competitive intelligence capture in C4C. Pricing decisions are made without systematic data on what competitors are offering.
  • Reporting requires manual data exports from C4C to Excel. The C4C standard reports do not cover all required KPIs, and ad-hoc reporting takes 2–4 hours per report.
